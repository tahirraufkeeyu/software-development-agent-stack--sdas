## Objection Handling

Three to five objections the prospect will raise. Each response follows **Acknowledge → Reframe → Redirect**.

### Objection 1: "[verbatim objection, e.g. 'Competitor X is cheaper']"

- **Acknowledge**: "It's true that their list price can look lower on paper."
- **Reframe**: "The comparison most customers end up making is total cost over 24 months, including implementation and the managed services required to keep it running. On that basis, Acme Retail found the delta inverted — they moved from Competitor X to us and cut total cost by 18%."
- **Redirect**: "What's your internal comparison framework — per-seat list or total cost?"

## Landmines

Discovery questions the buyer can ask the competitor (or themselves) that expose the competitor's weakness without us attacking directly. Phrase as questions the buyer would naturally ask.

1. "What is the median implementation time for a customer of our size and stack?"
2. "Can you share three references in our vertical who went live in the last 12 months?"
3. "What's your contract term, and is there a ramp-down clause if we reduce seats?"
4. "How do you handle [specific feature] — is it native or via integration?"
5. "What's the roadmap for [an area you know is weak]?"

## Do Not Say

A short list of phrases to avoid when this competitor is in the deal. Often legal-sensitive (trademark, disparagement) or strategically counterproductive.

- Do not say "they're going out of business" — even if rumored.
- Do not say "their CEO just left" as a buying reason — focus on product impact.
- Do not name-drop their customers we've lost unless the prospect names them first.
